Social Strategy · Content Formats
How We Post

Content Formats

Two formats only — carousels and static images. No video for now. Each has a specific role. Carousels dominate because they force interaction, and interaction signals quality to the algorithm.

60%
of all posts should be carousels — 3.1× higher engagement than static
40%
static images — faster to produce, best for punchy single-idea posts
4–7
slides per carousel max — one bold idea per slide, no cluttered layouts
Carousel Types
Carousel Format 1
PAS — Problem · Agitate · Solve

Most powerful format. Slide 1: bold hook. Slides 2–3: make it feel real and costly. Slides 4–5: solution reveal. Final slide: CTA to audit. Use for Pillars 1 & 2.

Carousel Format 2
List — "X things costing you jobs"

One insight per slide. EB Garamond header, Inter body, amber number as the anchor. Great for Pillar 3 (Industry Truth) — highly shareable and saveable.

Carousel Format 3
Before / After

2–3 slides. Slide 1: the problem state. Slide 2: the fixed state. No faces needed — use mockups, iconography, text overlay. Works for both website and automation service.

Carousel Format 4
How It Works

Explainer carousel — "Here's how we fix it in 72 hours." Clear step-by-step, no jargon. 4–6 slides. Good for Pillar 2 and converting warmer audiences.

Static Image Types
Static Type 1
The Bold Stat

One number, big, amber. Supporting context in smaller Inter. Dark charcoal background. "67% of customers research online before calling. What do they find when they look for you?"

Static Type 2
The Single Hook

Short sharp copy. EB Garamond italic for the hook, Inter for the follow-up line. "You missed 3 calls last week. Your competitor didn't." — no embellishment needed.
