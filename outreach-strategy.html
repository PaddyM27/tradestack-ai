Section 01

The Strategy at a Glance

The core insight is simple: not every business needs the same pitch. By sorting prospects into two groups before you contact them, every message is relevant — and relevance is what gets replies.

Prong 01
No / Poor Website

Who: Businesses on Google Maps with no website, a broken one, or something that looks like it was built in 2008.

Pain: Invisible to customers searching online. Losing jobs to competitors who show up.

Offer: A fast, mobile-first website that turns Google visitors into booked enquiries.

Hook: "Customers can't find you."

Prong 02
Has Website

Who: Businesses with a functioning, decent website — but almost certainly leaking leads after hours.

Pain: Website exists but leads go cold. No follow-up, no automation, no system.

Offer: AI-powered systems to respond, follow up, and convert leads automatically.

Hook: "You're leaking leads you don't know about."

Phase 01

Building Your Lead Lists

The quality of your outreach is only as good as your list. This phase is about building a clean, segmented prospect database you can work systematically — not spray-and-pray.

Step 1
Scrape with Outscraper

Go to outscraper.com and run searches for each trade category across your target counties. This pulls directly from Google Maps and returns business name, phone, email, website, reviews, and rating.

Search Format to Use

"plumber Dublin" · "electrician Cork" · "roofer Galway" · "painter Limerick" · "builder Waterford"
Repeat for every trade + county combination. Start with the 5 largest counties first.

  • Set results per search to 200–500 — enough to get started without overspending credits
  • Always export with: Name, Category, Phone, Email, Website, Reviews, Rating, Address
  • Outscraper costs roughly €10–20 to pull a few thousand leads — cheap for what you get
Step 2
Set Up Your Master Google Sheet

Paste all exports into one master Google Sheet and add these columns:

Column What to put there Used for
Business Name From Outscraper Personalisation field in Instantly
Owner First Name Find via website/Google/LinkedIn Personalisation — "Hi John"
Email From Outscraper or Hunter.io Sending address
Phone From Outscraper WhatsApp outreach
Website From Outscraper Segmentation + first line research
Prong 1 or 2 (after you check the website) Which sequence to send
First Line Your custom icebreaker sentence Paste into Instantly as a merge field
Status New / Sent / Replied / Booked Pipeline tracking
Step 3
Write Your Custom First Lines

This is the most important 20 minutes you'll spend per batch of leads. Open each website (or lack thereof) and write one sentence that proves you actually looked. Use this formula:

The Formula

[Specific observation about their business] + [Bridge to the pain]

  • Prong 1: "Noticed [Business Name] has 34 Google reviews but no website — a lot of customers won't call if they can't see your work first."
  • Prong 1: "Looked up [Business Name] on Google and the website link comes up as an error — easy thing to miss but it's costing you enquiries."
  • Prong 2: "Had a look at the [Business Name] site — looks solid, but I noticed there's no way to enquire after hours."
  • Prong 2: "Checked out [Business Name]'s site — most [trade] businesses in Ireland lose 4–6 jobs a month just from slow response times."
The Golden Rule

If the first line could be sent to anyone, rewrite it. It must be specific enough that the recipient knows you actually looked at their business.

Phase 02 · Prong 1

Email Sequence: No / Poor Website

Three emails over 10 days. Objective: start a reply. The pain is visible and obvious — your job is to make them feel it, not lecture them. Keep it short, conversational, and direct.

Email 1 — Initial Outreach Day 1
Subject: [Business Name] — quick question
[Custom first line — your specific observation about their business] Most [trade] businesses don't realise how many jobs this is costing them — customers who found them on Google but couldn't see their work, get a quote, or book in. We build fast, mobile-first websites for trade businesses — designed to turn Google visitors into booked jobs, not just browsers. Worth a quick chat to see if it'd make sense for you? — Paddy FlowBuild AI
Email 2 — Follow Up Day 5
Subject: Re: [Business Name] — quick question
Just circling back on this — I know you're probably flat out. One question: are you currently getting enquiries from Google, or is it mostly word of mouth? If it's mostly referrals, a website would open up a whole new stream. Happy to show you a few examples of what we've done for similar [trade] businesses. — Paddy
Email 3 — Break-up Day 10
Subject: Closing the loop
Not going to keep pestering you — just wanted to leave this here: If you ever want to get [Business Name] showing up properly online and converting enquiries, that's exactly what we do. Reply any time — no pressure. — Paddy
When They Reply

1. Acknowledge: "Makes sense — most [trade] owners don't think about it until they realise how many jobs competitors are getting from Google."
2. Qualify: "What does most of your work come from currently?"
3. Offer: "I can put together a quick look at what we'd build for [Business Name] — usually takes a day or two. Want me to do that?"

Phase 03

Multi-Channel Outreach

Email is your primary channel — but being present across LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and WhatsApp increases touchpoints and gets replies from people who never check their inbox. Layer these in after your email sequences are running.

Channel 1
LinkedIn

Search for business owners by trade + location. Send a connection request with a note (300 chars max), then message once accepted. Keep it one question — wait for a reply before saying anything about FlowBuild AI.

Connection Request Note 300 chars max
"Hi [Name] — came across [Business Name] while looking at [trade] businesses in [county]. Got an idea that might be worth 2 mins of your time. Happy to connect."
Message 1 — After Accepted
"Thanks for connecting [Name]. Quick question — are you finding most of your new jobs come through word of mouth, or are you getting much from Google / your website? Asking because there's usually a big gap there for [trade] businesses and I think I could show you something useful."
Channel 2
Instagram & Facebook DMs

Search by trade + county on both platforms to find business pages. Message the page directly — keep it extremely casual, like a text from a real person. One question only. Do not pitch on the first message.

DM Template — Instagram & Facebook
"Hey [Name] — love the work on your page. Quick question: are you getting many enquiries through here or mainly referrals? I help [trade] businesses in Ireland set up systems so they never miss a lead — just wondering if it's something you've ever looked at?"
  • Do not automate Instagram or Facebook DMs — accounts get banned and it kills credibility
  • Do this manually in 30-minute batches — 10–15 DMs per session is sustainable
Channel 3
WhatsApp / SMS

Only use when you have a mobile number from Outscraper. This is the most personal channel — treat it that way. Keep it short, send during business hours (9–5), and never follow up more than once.

WhatsApp Template
"Hi [Name], Paddy here from FlowBuild AI. I help [trade] businesses in Ireland stop losing leads to missed calls and slow follow-up. Would it be worth a quick chat? No pressure at all — just thought it might be relevant to [Business Name]."
Channel Priority

Start with email only in Week 1–2. Add LinkedIn in Week 2. Add Instagram/Facebook in Week 4. Add WhatsApp selectively for warm leads or high-priority prospects. Don't spread yourself thin before you know what's converting.

Phase 04

Launch Timeline

Roll out one channel at a time. Trying to run everything at once leads to chaos — validate email first, then layer in the other channels as you build momentum.

Week 1
Build lists · Launch Prong 1 email Scrape 200 leads from Dublin & Cork. Sort into Prong 1 vs 2. Write custom first lines for Prong 1 batch. Set up Instantly sequence and launch. Start domain warmup if not already running.
Week 2
Launch Prong 2 · Add LinkedIn Upload and launch Prong 2 email sequence. Start manual LinkedIn connection requests for the same lead list. Begin replying to any email responses — use the reply framework.
Week 3
Review · Expand geography Check reply rates for both prongs. Kill any subject lines under 3% after 100 sends and rewrite them. Scrape 200 more leads from Galway, Limerick, and Waterford. Keep the best-performing sequences running.
Week 4
Add social channels · Optimise Start Instagram and Facebook DM outreach in 30-min batches. Use WhatsApp selectively for your highest-priority leads. A/B test one subject line variable. Target: at least 1–2 live conversations by end of week.
Week 5+
Weekly cadence Scrape → segment → write first lines → send → reply → iterate. Scale channels and volume based on what's converting. Add GoHighLevel after first deal closes.

Benchmarks for context

The industry average for cold email is 1–3% reply rate. If you're hitting 5%+ with the smart hybrid approach, you're well ahead of the curve. Prong 1 will likely outperform Prong 2 early on — the pain is more urgent and the first line practically writes itself.

Instantly.ai Setup Checklist
  • Sending domain purchased and DNS configured (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
  • Email warmup running for minimum 2–3 weeks before first send
  • Two sequences built — one for Prong 1, one for Prong 2
  • Custom first line set as a personalisation variable (column in your import CSV)
  • Tags applied to every lead: Prong1-NoWebsite / Prong1-PoorWebsite / Prong2
  • Daily send limit set to max 50/day per inbox to stay out of spam

The Closing Thought
The system only works
if you actually run it.

Most people build the sequences, do one batch, get no instant results, and stop. The businesses that win at cold outreach treat it like a weekly habit — 2 hours on Monday to scrape and prep, sequences running in the background all week, replies handled daily. Volume and consistency beat any clever tactic.

The Weekly Habit
Monday: Scrape + segment + write first lines → Launch batch
Daily: Check replies, respond fast, move convos forward
Friday: Review metrics, kill what isn't working, plan next week
